Is It Skin Cancer?

Unusual blemishes and bumps might be an indicator melanoma or other types of skin cancer. Use this app to calculate the likelihood that a melanoma might be developing. Get Started

The following code has been developed by students and/or researchers of the Freshman Research Initiative DIY Diagnostics Stream at The University of Texas at Austin. This code is shared for demonstration purposes and should not be considered a product -- it is for entertainment purposes only. Any user of this code does so at their own risk. Members of the DIY Stream, FRI, and The University of Texas system are not liable for anything related to this code.

THIS CODE SHOULD NOT BE USED TO DIAGNOSE ANY KIND OF MEDICAL CONDITION.

Asymmetry

Is the blemish asymetrical?

It is asymmentrical if one half of the mole or birthmark does not match the other half.

Borders

Are the borders of the blemish abnormal or blurred?

Color

Is the color even?

If the blemish color is uneven with varying shades, select "No"

Diameter

Measure the approximate diameter (length from edge to edge, passing through the center) of the blemish/mole.

Is this diameter larger than 6mm? This is

*note: 6 mm ~ 0.25 inch or about the size of an eraser on the back of a pencil.

Yes No

Evolving

Have you noticed the blemish changing in either size, shape, or color?

Results

See your results Note that not all melanomas follow the rules of ABCD. It is important not any new appearances and to see a doctor for accurate testing.

Other possible factors of melanoma to keep note of include:

  • spreading
  • itchiness
  • tenderness
  • pain (not in all cases)
  • oozing
  • bleeding
  • presence of a lump
